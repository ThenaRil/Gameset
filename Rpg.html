History.

Racing games have been one of the foundations for video games since its early days. The first racing game that was made was the Gran Trak 10 (1974), a coin-operated arcade game made by Atari. But then followed by Pole Position (1982), which opened up much more complex mechanics and is often credited with defining the genre for its time.

As devices keep on advancing, so did the advancements of graphics in video games. The rise of both arcade-style and simulation-based racing games. Game titles like Virtua Racing (1992) and Daytona USA (1993), these games offered 3D graphics and multiplayer modes. Meanwhile, games like Gran Turismo elevated the racing simulation scene, providing a high degree of realism in terms of car handling, physics, and track design.

Infographics.

The racing game genre has evolved significantly over the decades. It began in the 1970s with simple arcade games like Gran Trak 10, focusing on basic driving mechanics. The 1990s introduced iconic titles such as Mario Kart, blending fun with competition, and Need for Speed, which added thrilling car chases. In the 2000s, franchises like Forza Motorsport and Gran Turismo pushed realism with detailed graphics and car physics. Today, advancements like virtual reality, dynamic weather, and open-world exploration in games like Forza Horizon and F1 23 showcase cutting-edge technology, delivering immersive and lifelike racing experiences.

Gameplay Mechanics.

Gran Turismo 7 offers a simulation-style driving experience with a strong emphasis on realism, car collection, and customization. Players race on a variety of tracks with a vast selection of cars, ranging from classic vehicles to modern high-performance models. The game features realistic driving physics, including weather effects, tire wear, and vehicle handling that require skillful control. Players can engage in single-player career modes, compete online, and participate in time trials or races against AI opponents. The game also includes a robust tuning system for customizing car performance and aesthetics.
